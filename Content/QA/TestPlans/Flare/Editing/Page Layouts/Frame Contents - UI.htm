Frame Contents Pane - UI

Opening the Frame Contents pane:

  • !Tools | Page Frame Contents Pane
  • Open a Page Layout file, select a Header, Footer or Decoration frame and:
    • Press <F2> (If the frame is empty, you will be prompted to pick a content template. Click No to open the Frame Contents pane.)
    • Click the "Edit Text" button in the Local Toolbar (If the frame is empty, you will be prompted to pick a content template. Click No to open the Frame Contents pane.)

 

Frame Contents pane:

Default view docked in the lower pane:

Title Bar displays the filename of the Page Layout file to which the frame belongs followed (in parentheses) by the page type and flow.

Title Bar displays dirty (with an *) when a change is made.

 

Local Toolbar

Local Toolbar Buttons (Tooltips in parentheses):

  • (Save file): Saves changes made to the Frame Contents.
  • (Reload the file): Returns frame content to its previous state, discarding any unsaved changes if you instruct it to.
  • (Medium): Clicking the down arrow on this button lets you select a medium so that you can view the frame contents with the styles from a specific medium.
  • (Insert a picture): Opens the Insert Picture dialog which lets you insert an image at the location of the cursor.

  • (Quick character): Clicking the button inserts the Quick Character. Clicking the triangle reveals the Insert Character sub-menu.

    Insert Character sub-menu:

    • Select 'Quick Character': Opens the Quick Character dialog, which lets you specify a quick character. That character or symbol will then be associated with the Quick Character button.
    • Quick Character: Inserts the quick character into the topic.
    • Non-breaking Space: Inserts a non-breaking space into the editor.
    • Copyright: Inserts a copyright symbol (©) into the editor.
    • Registered Trademark: Inserts a registered trademark symbol (®) into the editor.
    • Trademark: Inserts a trademark symbol (™) into the editor.
    • Degree: Inserts a degree symbol (°) into the editor.
    • Bullet: Inserts a round black bullet (•) into the editor.
    • Double Dagger: Inserts a double dagger symbol (‡) into the editor.
    • Ellipses: Inserts an ellipsis (…) into the editor.
    • N Dash: Inserts an n-dash (–), or en dash, into the editor.
    • M Dash: Inserts an m-dash (—), or em dash, into the editor.
    • Plus/Minus: Inserts a plus/minus symbol (±) into the editor.
    • Greater or Equal: Inserts a combined "greater or equal" symbol (≥) into the editor.
    • Not Equal: Inserts a "not equal" symbol (≠) into the editor.
    • Cent: Inserts a cent monetary symbol (¢) into the editor.
    • Euro: Inserts a euro monetary symbol (€) into the editor.
    • Pound: Inserts a pound monetary symbol (£) into the editor.
    • Yen: Inserts a yen monetary symbol (¥) into the editor.
  • (Toggle show space): Turns paragraph and space markers on and off.

  • (Show tags): Clicking the button changes the content view, showing the XML tags surrounding the content. Clicking the triangle reveals the Show sub-menu:

    Show sub-menu:

    • Show Caret Tag Neighborhood: Turns this feature on and off.
    • Show Markers: Turns markers on or off in the active editor.
    • Show Inline Markers: You can show or hide markers for all inline elements in the active editor.
    • Show invisible tags: Turns invisible tags (e.g., <title>) on or off in the active editor. This option is enabled only if you have selected "Show tags."
    • Show Comments: Turns comment tags on or off in the active editor.
    • Show All Images As Thumbnails: Lets you show or hide images in your project as thumbnails.
    • Show tags in mono-color: Lets you choose whether to display tags in a color or in black. This option is enabled only if you have selected "Show tags."
    • Show tags in mono-font: Lets you choose whether to display all tags in the same font or in the font actually applied to each area. This option is enabled only if you have selected "Show tags."
    • Enable Object Positioning: Turns object positioning on and off.
    • Enable Orphan/Widow Control: Turns widow and orphan control on or off.
    • Show Hidden Elements: Shows content that has been designated as "hidden."
    • Show Conditional Indicators: If you have created and applied condition tags, clicking this button toggles between showing and hiding them.
    • Show Variable Names: Lets you choose whether to see the names of variables in your markers (if you have inserted variables) in the active editor.
    • Highlight Variables: Lets you choose whether to see a gray background behind variable definitions in the editor.
    • Show Bookmark Names: Lets you choose whether to see the names of bookmarks in your markers (if you have inserted bookmarks) in the active editor.
    • Show Named Elements: Lets you choose whether to see the yellow flag icon next to named elements in your markers (if you have inserted named elements) in the active editor.
    • Locks (Disabled/Enabled): Locks or unlocks the content designated with the gray background.
    • Show Locked Elements: Allows you to show or hide the gray background associated with elements that have been locked or selected to be locked.
    • Show Annotations: Displays any annotations (notes) that have been attached to any content in the editor.
    • Marker Width: Lets you adjust the width of markers in the active topic to see more or less of the markers when they are visible.

 

Lower Toolbar

Local Toolbar Buttons (Tooltips in parentheses):

  • Words: [word count]Displays the number of words in this frame
  • (Smaller Font): Reduces the scale of the font by 10% each time you click the button.
  • (Reset font scaling to 100%): Resets the scale of the font to 100%.
  • (Larger Font): Increases the scale of the font by 10% each time you click the button.
  • : Lets you select a specific percentage to scale the font.
  • (Hide/show Conditional Indicators): Lets you show or hide the colored shading of content to which condition tags have been applied.
  • (Toggle show blocks): Lets you show or hide the tag block bars.
  • (Toggle show spans): Lets you show or hide the span bars.
  • (Toggle show the horizontal ruler): Lets you show or hide a measurement ruler horizontally at the top of the editor or window pane.
  • (Toggle show the vertical ruler): Lets you show or hide a measurement ruler vertically at the left of the editor or window pane.
